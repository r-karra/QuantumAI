## Notes
**Introduction: Self-teach Physics? (Karra, 2024)**

**References**
Karra, R. K. (2024, February 9th). Learn Physics on your own. Wikipedia. Retrieved February 11, 2024, from [https://gemini.google.com/share/1d341ca36c37](https://gemini.google.com/share/1d341ca36c37)

Self-teaching physics is definitely possible and rewarding! Here are some resources and tips to get you started:

1.  **Define your goals:**
    * What areas of physics interest you? (Mechanics, electromagnetism, optics, etc.)
    * What depth of understanding do you aim for? (Basic concepts, problem-solving, advanced theory)
    * How much time can you dedicate to studying each week?
2.  **Choose your resources:**
    * **Textbooks:** Popular options include *"Fundamentals of Physics"* by Halliday, Resnick, and Walker, or *"Physics for Scientists and Engineers"* by Tipler and Mosca. Choose based on your level and goals.
    * **Online Courses:** MIT OpenCourseware, Khan Academy, and edX offer free physics courses with video lectures, practice problems, and exams.
    * **YouTube Channels:** Channels like SciShow Physics, PBS Space Time, and Veritasium explain complex concepts in engaging ways.
    * **Apps and Games:** Interactive apps like *"Newton's Playground"* and games like *"Kerbal Space Program"* can make learning fun and practical.
3.  **Develop a study schedule:**
    * Set realistic goals for each week or month.
    * Alternate between reading, watching lectures, and doing practice problems.
    * Don't be afraid to ask questions online or find a study partner.
4.  **Make it practical:**
    * Look for real-world applications of the physics you're learning.
    * Try hands-on activities and experiments to solidify your understanding.
    * Join online communities or forums to discuss physics with others.

**Additional tips:**
* Focus on understanding concepts, not just memorizing formulas.
* Don't be afraid to make mistakes and learn from them.
* Be patient and persistent. Learning physics takes time and effort.

**Here are some extra resources that you might find helpful:**
* **Books:** *"How to Teach Yourself Physics"* by Richard Feynman, *"Six Ideas That Shaped Physics"* by Steven Weinberg
* **Websites:** The Physics Classroom, HyperPhysics, The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
* **Podcasts:** The Infinite Monkey Cage, Physics World Weekly

Remember, the key to successful self-learning is discipline, curiosity, and a passion for the subject. Good luck on your physics journey!
